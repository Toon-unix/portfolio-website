DumperBumper

DumperBumper is a hands-on project page for experiments, problem solving, and iterating on something useful.

A practical project focused on learning by doing, with a clear problem to solve and a tangible outcome to share.

Overview

We got tasked with designing, testing and making a hosting platform for our internal client. For now the project consists of logging in, where you can then log in. Logged in you can see all your projects you have already made. Afterwards you can click to make a new project, where you can upload HTML (for now at least) and you can follow along where the file is in its upload stage. File uploaded? Then click the "visit website" button which appears to take you straight to your hosted website inside of a kubernetes container. The primary and two secondary kubernetes cluster being on seperate VMs. This project is still in development, but it already has taught me so so much, such as how to lead a team, which I have never done before. It's a quite exciting projects, especially when you get to see your teammates work on it I get glee in my eyes.

Background

This project was made as part of the “IoT Essentials” course at Thomas More University of Applied Sciences. As one of the first group projects we worked on, we were placed together with other students following the same course. Luckily, my team quickly got to know each other, and before long, we were coordinating like a real team. My role focused on setting up AWS and the FastAPI backend to retrieve images from the bucket, make them available online, and send them to our frontend.
