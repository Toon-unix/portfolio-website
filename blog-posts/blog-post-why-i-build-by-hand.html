Why I Keep Building by Hand In The Age Of AI

29 Mar 2025

In the age of AI, building anything, not just websites seems to be getting easier and easier, so why would anyone still build anything by hand? Why put in the blood, sweat and tears that come from building and failing over and over again when you can have it right on the first try?

Well, dear reader, I am a firm believer of learning to walk by falling, what do I mean by that? Well, if you walk from the first try, you might know how to walk the right way, but might not know what happens if you don't put that foot down infront of you, AI takes away the crucial learning aspect from walking, or in this case, writing code, or doing anything. Of course, I know there are many practicalities with AI, I remember when they were testing AI in the medical field, and if it can get better, that would be a huge win for all of humanity! I just do not believe AI has a place in the learning context, not getting critisism for your work because an AI wrote it, might feel good in the moment but you have not learned anything.

> Today I learned
3rd of April 2026
The "mains humm" you hear coming from powerlines, especially in Europe, is actually due to the alternating current flowing through them, it changes direction 50 times per second. Of course this isn't super efficient and a little bit of that vibration becomes sound at that 50Hz. If you record something with "mains humm" in it, it is therefore possible to see when something was recorded.
